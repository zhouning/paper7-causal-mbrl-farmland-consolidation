Dear Editor,

We are pleased to submit our manuscript entitled "A Treatment-Effect-Informed Learned Environment for Reinforcement Learning-Based Farmland Consolidation Planning" for consideration as a Research Article in Computers, Environment and Urban Systems.

The manuscript addresses a practical geospatial decision-support problem: how county-level planning authorities can use reinforcement learning for farmland consolidation when the real planning environment is expensive to simulate and model-free training is unstable. We study this problem using all 13 townships of Bishan District, Chongqing, comprising 2,600 consolidation blocks and 52,515 parcels.

The central contribution is a model-based geocomputational framework that learns a lightweight transition model from real planning trajectories and uses it as a CPU-based learned environment for policy optimization. The manuscript further introduces treatment-effect-informed reward calibration, using observational trajectory evidence to regularize reward overestimation in the learned environment.

We believe the paper is well aligned with CEUS because it combines geocomputation, artificial intelligence, spatial planning support, and land/resource management. The work is framed as a planning-support method for large-scale land consolidation rather than as a generic reinforcement-learning benchmark, with emphasis on computational feasibility, reward-exploitation failure modes, and real-environment evaluation.

Specifically, the manuscript reports:

1. A learned-environment formulation for county-level farmland consolidation with 2,600 block actions and a 44,212-dimensional state representation.

2. A 237K-parameter transition model trained from 12,000 real-environment transitions.

3. A treatment-effect-informed reward calibration method checked against an eight-value reward-scaling grid.

4. A 15-seed real-environment evaluation showing that calibrated model-based policies improve over the uncalibrated learned-environment policy under an exact paired sign-flip test, with descriptive comparison to GPU-trained model-free baselines.

5. Policy-induced learned-vs-real diagnostics, a trajectory-source robustness ablation, and a Dongxing District full-reward local counterpart experiment covering full baselines, a local learned policy, one-step model-based action selection, held-out scoring optimization, and a multi-step learned-environment policy, with explicit limits on direct cross-county transfer claims.

The manuscript has not been published previously and is not under consideration elsewhere. All authors have approved the submission. The authors declare no competing interests. Data and code availability are described in the title-page declarations and can be provided for peer review in anonymized form subject to the applicable data-use constraints.
